Used only real time, not user or sys time. O time is 

sort1 uses: Bubble Sort

How do you know?: Sorted (best case) with bubble sort means it will require much less time
in comparison to random or reversed sorts. The difference between a sorted list of 50k items
and 50k items in random or reversed order being about 5 seconds is the tell. No theta, or O
and Omega time complexity being the same, here.

sort2 uses: Merge Sort

How do you know?: The quickest of the sorting methods on average. Of nine tests, the only one
to go above 0.5 seconds was sorted 50k. Even then, only 0.523s. Plus, all 50k lists were all
similar in time taken, range of 0.067s, meaning theta does exist here. And this theta being the
smallest, or least time, makes me conclude that of the three, this one is merge.

sort3 uses: Selection Sort

How do you know?: By process of elimination, sort3 is selection sort. Seems to have a theta since
small range in 50k lists times, 0.318s, so best and worst case have similar time complexity. And,
the greater time taken in the 50k list, in general when compared to sort2, means the time complexity
of this sort type is greater than the time complexity of sort2, which we concluded was merge sort,
with O(n log n). So, which of the three methods has a theta time complexity which is slower than the rest?
Selection sort.
